## 4. Filing Basis

**Section 1(b) — Intent to Use**

The applicant has a bona fide intention to use the mark in commerce in connection with the goods and services identified above. The mark is not yet in use in interstate commerce at the time of filing.

> **Why Intent to Use:** Establishes priority date from filing date. Applicant has 6 months from Notice of Allowance to file Statement of Use (Form 8), extendable in 6-month increments up to 36 months total. Convert to use-based registration upon first commercial transaction under the mark.

> **Alternative — Section 1(a) Use in Commerce:** If the mark is already appearing on the company website, marketing materials, or any commercial transaction (including a quote, invoice, or contract), it may qualify as use in commerce. In that case, file under 1(a) and include a specimen (screenshot of mark on website header, business card, or product packaging). Filing under 1(a) is slightly stronger — no Statement of Use conversion required.

## 7. Attorney Recommendation

Retain a USPTO-registered trademark attorney or agent for final review before filing. Recommended focus areas for counsel:

1. **Confirm class descriptions** pass USPTO examiner review — Class 35 descriptions in particular are frequently rejected for vagueness; counsel can refine language to match accepted ID Manual entries
2. **Conduct full TESS search** (USPTO Trademark Electronic Search System) covering phonetic equivalents and design marks before filing — confirm no registered marks for AEGLOS or phonetic variants (AGLOS, EAGLOS, etc.) exist in Classes 9, 35, or 42
3. **Review the Meaning/Significance declaration** — the acronym statement is the load-bearing element of the IP defense strategy; counsel should verify the language is sufficient to establish independent origin on the record
4. **File as soon as possible** — USPTO priority is first-to-file; the search showing no current registrations is a narrow window, not a permanent condition
