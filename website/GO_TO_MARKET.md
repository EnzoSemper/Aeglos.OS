## Why These Three

These products were selected based on four criteria: existing OEM infrastructure, regulatory simplicity, immediate market demand, and brand coherence with Aeglos Systems' defense positioning. All three can realistically move from conversation to product in hand within 6–12 months without requiring new tooling investment, FDA pathways longer than 90 days, or ITAR licensing.

## 1. BALOR IFAK
**Category:** Combat Medical | **Line:** Balor Medical

### The Opportunity
Individual First Aid Kits are consumed in massive quantities across DoD, law enforcement, first responder, and civilian preparedness markets. TCCC-compliant IFAKs are a near-mandatory purchase for any serious professional or unit. There is no dominant brand in the private-label space — the market is currently dominated by the underlying component manufacturers (North American Rescue, Chinook Medical) selling directly under their own names. A strong brand with a compelling story and professional packaging has real room to differentiate.

### Assembly Model

**In-house solo assembly.** No third-party labor. Owner packs, seals, and ships direct. This eliminates $3–$5/unit assembly cost and gives full QC control. Feasible at launch volumes (50–300 units/month) without additional staff.

**Step 3 — Branding: Aeglos Systems Patches (Month 1)**

PVC velcro-backed patches are the right call over embroidered logos or printed labels. They're waterproof, more durable in field conditions, interchangeable across pouch styles, and require no custom pouch tooling. A patch inventory also decouples branding from pouch inventory — you can change pouch suppliers without retiring any branding assets.

**Patch spec:**
- Size: **3" × 2"** (standard IFAK panel size, fits Condor and BFG pouches)
- Material: **PVC rubber** — preferred over embroidered for medical/field gear; holds color, waterproof, no fraying
- Design: Aeglos Systems logo (front face) + "BALOR MEDICAL" text below
- Backing: **Hook velcro** (attaches to loop velcro panel on pouch front)
- Color: Coyote Tan base with subdued logo, or full-color option for direct/civilian sales; IR-reflective variant as a premium add for military/LE channel

**Step 4 — Assembly workflow (in-house)**

Per kit, in order:
1. Lay pouch flat, open main compartment
2. Pack contents in sequence: gloves first (bottom), gauze ×2, NPA + lube, chest seal, shears, marker
3. CAT tourniquet in external TQ sleeve or inside main compartment per spec
4. Zip and close
5. Press Aeglos Systems PVC patch onto front velcro panel — firm contact, edges flush
6. Inspect and seal in a clear poly bag with label (see Step 5)
7. Box for shipment

Average pack time per kit once practiced: **4–6 minutes.** At 50 kits in a session, expect a half-day of work. At 100 kits/month this is a 1–2 day monthly commitment.

**Step 5 — Label and exterior packaging**

Each sealed kit needs an exterior label covering:
- Product name: Balor IFAK
- Lot number / date of assembly (for recall traceability — required as FDA establishment)
- Contents list
- QR code linking to TCCC instruction video (host on aeglossystems.com)
- "For professional use — see enclosed instructions"
- Aeglos Systems name and address

Print labels via **Sticker Mule** (stickermule.com) or **Avery** label stock on a laser printer. Sticker Mule at 100 labels: ~$60–$80. Avery on laser: ~$0.10–$0.20/label.

Clear poly bag for sealed kit: **Uline S-series poly bags**, size 9" × 12" — ~$0.08–$0.12/bag at case quantity.

**Step 5 — Regulatory**

All components are FDA-registered Class I or II devices. As a kit assembler/repackager, Aeglos Systems would register as an FDA Medical Device Establishment (510(k) not required for kit assembly of cleared components). Registration fee: $6,493/year (2026 rate). File Form FDA 2891. Timeline: ~30 days.

## 2. ELESSAR MULTI-TOOL PRO
**Category:** EDC | **Line:** Corvus EDC

The tactical multi-tool market is enormous and sticky — buyers are loyal, gifting is common, and unit economics are favorable. The private-label angle works well here because Leatherman is already trusted by every serious operator; Aeglos branding on a Leatherman-quality platform immediately borrows that credibility while building the Corvus identity. The co-branding risk is minimal because the tool is sold as Aeglos/Corvus — the OEM relationship does not need to be publicized.

## 3. DURENDAL MK-1 FIXED BLADE
**Category:** Edged Weapons | **Line:** Durendal

### The Opportunity
The tactical fixed blade market rewards heritage, story, and steel quality above all else. "Durendal" carries immediate weight — Roland's legendary sword from the Song of Roland, the 11th-century French chanson de geste. It is public domain, carries no IP liability, and resonates strongly with the military and defense buyer who values historical depth. A high-quality American-made 8" fixed blade with premium steel, a strong origin story, and serious construction can command $200–$400 at retail and build genuine following. This is a brand-anchor product — buyers keep these for decades and talk about them.

### Procurement Path

**Step 1 — Identify Production Partner (Month 1–2)**

The decision here is between two fundamentally different strategies:

**Strategy A: Custom American Production (recommended)**

Target maker: **ESEE Knives (Randall's Adventure & Training)**
- Address: 5252 W State Road 144, Gallatin, TN 37066
- Contact: (615) 452-5656 / info@eseeknives.com
- ESEE has built their reputation on field-worthy fixed blades and has worked with external buyers on custom production runs. Their 1095 carbon steel heat treatment process (RC 55-57) is well-documented and trusted. Request a meeting to discuss a Durendal-branded production run — their staff are approachable and used to custom conversations.
- What to bring to the conversation: a sketch/spec sheet with blade geometry, overall dimensions, steel preference, handle material, and target price point. They will quote a per-unit price at various volumes.
- Realistic MOQ for ESEE custom: **250–500 units**
- Expected unit cost at 500 units: **$65–$95 depending on steel and handle complexity**

Alternative A2: **Bark River Knives**
- Address: 5855 Delta Ave, Escanaba, MI 49829
- Contact: (906) 789-1139 / info@barkriverknives.com
- Bark River does smaller custom runs and uses premium handle materials (stabilized wood, Micarta). Higher per-unit cost (~$95–$130) but exceptional fit and finish. Better if positioning Durendal as a premium collector/presentation piece rather than a field utility knife.

Alternative A3: **Direct custom smithing**
- For a true flagship presentation piece (limited edition, numbered), engage individual bladesmiths such as those listed with the **American Bladesmith Society (ABS)** (mailing: P.O. Box 977, Peralta, NM 87042). A Master Smith can produce a run of 50–100 presentation pieces at $300–$500 each that retail for $800–$1,200. Separate SKU, not the primary volume play.

**Strategy B: Offshore Production with US QC**

For higher volume at lower COGS:
- **Maxim Defense / Benchmade OEM:** Benchmade (Oregon City, OR) does not officially OEM, but their supply chain in the Pacific Northwest can point toward quality domestic alternatives
- **Taiwan production:** Companies like **FOX Knives** (Italian brand, manufactured in Taiwan) or directly through Taiwanese factories such as **Kuo Lung Knife** or **C.R.K.T.** (Columbia River Knife & Tool — Tualatin, OR — they contract manufacture globally and may facilitate introductions)
- Expected COGS offshore: $22–$38/unit at 1,000 units for a solid fixed blade
- Tradeoff: "Made in USA" story is lost; some buyers will care

**Recommendation:** Start with ESEE for 250–500 units to establish the product, build reviews and reputation. Scale to a secondary production facility (offshore or larger domestic) once demand is proven.

**Step 2 — Steel and Material Specification (Month 2)**

Recommended steel: **CPM-3V** (Crucible Industries, Syracuse, NY)
- Optimal for a field tactical knife: excellent toughness, edge retention, and resistance to chipping under hard use
- Order through **Admiral Steel** (Alsip, IL) or **Texas Knifemaker's Supply** (Houston, TX) if managing material supply independently
- If working with ESEE, they will source 1095 (their standard) — you can request 3V at an upcharge if they are tooled for it

Handle material (primary option): **Black Canvas Micarta**
- Source: Norplex-Micarta (Postville, IA) — domestic manufacturer
- G10 as second option (same supplier or Fibre-Tech Inc., Spring Valley, WI)
- Request handle slabs pre-cut to rough dimensions; ESEE will machine to final shape

Kydex sheath: **0.093" Kydex**
- Source: Kydex LLC (Lincolnshire, IL)
- Have ESEE produce or commission a sheath maker such as **Armatus Carry** (Chattanooga, TN) for custom Kydex work

**Step 3 — Legal and Regulatory (Month 1, parallel)**

Fixed blades are largely federally unregulated (no federal blade length restriction on fixed blades for adults). Key considerations:
- Carry restrictions vary by state — include a warning insert in packaging
- Export: fixed blades >5" may require EAR99 classification check; engage an export compliance consultant for international sales
- No federal licensing required to sell knives; standard business license + resale permits apply
- Trademark "Durendal": conduct a USPTO trademark search before filing. "Durendal" derives from the Old French chanson de geste (Song of Roland, ~1040–1115 AD) — public domain; no Tolkien Estate concerns. The name is not currently registered as a trademark in Class 8 (hand tools/cutlery). File for trademark registration in Class 8 before product launch to establish priority.

**Step 4 — Presentation and Packaging**

This product deserves premium unboxing:
- Matte black rigid box, magnetic closure, debossed Aeglos Systems logo
- Interior: custom foam bed cut to blade profile
- Included: certificate of authenticity (numbered if limited run), care/sharpening card, Aeglos Systems brand story card, microfiber pouch
- Outer sleeve with product photography

Packaging supplier: **Packlane** for prototype and short run; **Bert-Co** (Los Angeles, CA) or **Multi-Color Corporation** for volume
